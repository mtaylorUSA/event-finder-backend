# Visual Domain Taxonomy v0.1

---

## Purpose
     * This document defines a reusable **Visual Domain Taxonomy** for generating consistent, abstract, editorial-style images across sensitive national security, geopolitical, and global topics.
     * Domains constrain *how* visual metaphors are rendered, independent of topic semantics.
     * All content is proprietary and subject to the project owner’s copyright.

---

## Core Principles
     * Topics select **visual domains**; domains govern imagery.
     * One dominant visual metaphor per image.
     * Low-to-moderate density by default.
     * Abstract, symbolic representation only.
     * Ethical, non-graphic, non-sensational visuals.

---

## 1. TERRESTRIAL / ENVIRONMENTAL
     * **Use When**
          ** Climate change
          ** Natural resources
          ** Energy transition
          ** Environmental risk

     * **Visual Language**
          ** Atmosphere, sky, land, water, terrain
          ** Earth viewed from within atmosphere or as background context
          ** Sunlight, haze, smoke, weather systems
          ** Industry integrated into landscape

     * **Allowed Abstractions**
          ** Luminous continent outlines
          ** Latitude / longitude lines
          ** Airflow, heat, emissions as terrestrial flows

     * **Explicit Exclusions**
          ** ❌ Space, stars, galaxies, nebulae
          ** ❌ Orbits, halos, planetary rings
          ** ❌ Deep-space gradients or cosmic voids

     * **Defaults**
          ** Density: Low–Medium
          ** Contrast: Medium–High
          ** Humans: None

---

## 2. INSTITUTIONAL / GOVERNANCE
     * **Use When**
          ** Policy
          ** Diplomacy
          ** Alliances
          ** Treaties
          ** National security governance

     * **Visual Language**
          ** Architecture, chambers, civic spaces
          ** Order, symmetry, restraint
          ** Light interacting with structure

     * **Allowed Abstractions**
          ** Structural geometry
          ** Balance and alignment motifs

     * **Explicit Exclusions**
          ** ❌ Campaign imagery
          ** ❌ Crowds or rallies
          ** ❌ Flags unless country explicitly specified
          ** ❌ Named politicians or political parties

     * **Defaults**
          ** Density: Low
          ** Contrast: Medium
          ** Humans: Minimal, symbolic only

---

## 3. SYSTEMS / ORCHESTRATION
     * **Use When**
          ** Generative or agentic AI
          ** Cyber defense
          ** Intelligence systems
          ** Command-and-control (abstract)

     * **Visual Language**
          ** Nodes, layers, boundaries, flows
          ** Non-anthropomorphic AI cores
          ** Containment and oversight structures

     * **Allowed Abstractions**
          ** Frameworks and pathways
          ** Architectural systems logic

     * **Explicit Exclusions**
          ** ❌ Hackers, hoodies, laptops
          ** ❌ Literal code or readable interfaces
          ** ❌ Chaotic cyber aesthetics

     * **Defaults**
          ** Density: Low–Medium
          ** Contrast: High
          ** Humans: None or oversight silhouettes only

---

## 4. INDUSTRIAL / TECHNOLOGICAL
     * **Use When**
          ** Manufacturing
          ** Drones
          ** Weapons platforms (non-violent)
          ** Supply chains
          ** Energy systems

     * **Visual Language**
          ** Factories, machinery, infrastructure
          ** Mechanical geometry and scale

     * **Allowed Abstractions**
          ** Schematics
          ** Production and energy flows

     * **Explicit Exclusions**
          ** ❌ Combat scenes
          ** ❌ Explosions
          ** ❌ Futuristic sci-fi machinery

     * **Defaults**
          ** Density: Medium
          ** Contrast: High
          ** Humans: None or small-scale operators

---

## 5. GEOPOLITICAL / STRATEGIC
     * **Use When**
          ** War or conflict (non-graphic)
          ** Deterrence
          ** Strategic competition
          ** Regional instability

     * **Visual Language**
          ** Maps, borders, terrain
          ** Strategic abstraction and tension

     * **Allowed Abstractions**
          ** Zones and gradients of control
          ** Directional forces

     * **Explicit Exclusions**
          ** ❌ Gore or violence
          ** ❌ Active combat scenes
          ** ❌ Victim or suffering imagery

     * **Defaults**
          ** Density: Low–Medium
          ** Contrast: Medium–High
          ** Humans: None

---

## 6. NETWORK / TRANSNATIONAL
     * **Use When**
          ** Globalization
          ** Trade
          ** International norms
          ** Cyber norms
          ** Multilateral cooperation

     * **Visual Language**
          ** Flows between regions
          ** Subtle globe or world map in background

     * **Allowed Abstractions**
          ** Exchange corridors
          ** Connectivity patterns

     * **Explicit Exclusions**
          ** ❌ Space imagery
          ** ❌ Dominant foreground globe
          ** ❌ Flag clutter

     * **Defaults**
          ** Density: Low
          ** Contrast: Medium
          ** Humans: None

---

## 7. HUMANITARIAN / SOCIETAL
     * **Use When**
          ** Migration
          ** Refugees
          ** Public health
          ** Civil resilience

     * **Visual Language**
          ** Human-scale environments
          ** Grounded, empathetic framing

     * **Allowed Abstractions**
          ** Community systems
          ** Care and support pathways

     * **Explicit Exclusions**
          ** ❌ Trauma exploitation
          ** ❌ Graphic suffering
          ** ❌ Political theater

     * **Defaults**
          ** Density: Low
          ** Contrast: Medium
          ** Humans: Allowed but restrained

---

## 8. SPACE / ORBITAL (Explicit Opt-In Only)
     * **Use When**
          ** Space policy
          ** Satellites
          ** Astronomy

     * **Visual Language**
          ** Orbits, stars, vacuum
          ** Space hardware

     * **Critical Rule**
          ** This domain must be explicitly selected and is never inferred automatically.

---

## Global Cross-Domain Rules
     * One dominant metaphor per image
     * Secondary elements must be atmospheric
     * No readable text
     * No named U.S. politicians
     * No U.S. political parties
     * Ethical and non-sensational imagery only
